Section A: Multiple Choice Numericals

  1. A physical quantity P is related to four observables a, b, c, and d as P = a3 b2 / (c1/2 d). The percentage errors in measurements of a, b, c, and d are 1%, 3%, 4%, and 2% respectively. What is the percentage error in P?
    1. 10%
    2. 11%
    3. 13%
    4. 15%
  2. The period of oscillation of a simple pendulum is given by T = 2π√(L/g). The measured value of L is 20.0 cm known to 1 mm accuracy, and the time for 100 oscillations is 90 s using a wristwatch of 1 s resolution. The percentage error in g is:
    1. 1.5%
    2. 2.7%
    3. 3.2%
    4. 4.1%
  3. If velocity (v), force (F), and energy (E) are taken as the fundamental units, what is the dimensional formula for mass?
    1. E v-1
    2. E v-2
    3. F v-1
    4. F v-2
  4. In the Vander Waal's equation (P + a/V2)(V - b) = RT, what is the dimension of the product ab?
    1. [M L8 T-2]
    2. [M L5 T-2]
    3. [M L2 T-2]
    4. [M L8 T-1]
  5. The density of a material in CGS is 8 g cm-3. In a system where unit length is 5 cm and unit mass is 20 g, the density is:
    1. 10
    2. 25
    3. 40
    4. 50

Section B: Advanced Numericals

  1. The length, breadth, and thickness of a sheet are 4.234 m, 1.005 m, and 2.01 cm. Calculate area and volume to correct significant figures.
  2. Two resistances R1 = 100 ± 3 Ω and R2 = 200 ± 4 Ω are in parallel. Find the equivalent resistance with error.
  3. The refractive index of water is 1.29, 1.33, 1.34, 1.35, 1.32, 1.36, 1.30, 1.33. Calculate mean, mean absolute, and percentage error.
  4. For X = Ma Lb Tc, derive the expression for maximum percentage error given α, β, and γ.
  5. Find dimensions of a/b in F = a√x + bt2.
  6. Deduce dimensions of A, B, C, and D in v = A + Bt + C/(D+t).
  7. Determine the dimensional formula and SI unit of the Universal Gravitational Constant G.
  8. Check the accuracy of ν = (1 / 2L) √(T / m) using dimensional analysis.
  9. Derive an expression for the time period T of a pendulum using the method of dimensions.
  10. Prove dimensionally that λ ∝ h / (m v) for a moving particle.
  11. Derive the formula for volume rate of flow V through a capillary tube using dimensional analysis.
  12. Evaluate (25.2 × 1374) / 33.3 applying significant figure rules.
  13. Radius of a sphere is 2.1 ± 0.5 cm. Calculate surface area with error limits.
  14. Travel distance Δx = 13.8 ± 0.2 m in t = 4.0 ± 0.3 s. Calculate velocity v with error.
  15. Given 1/f = 1/u + 1/v with u = 30.0 ± 0.1 cm and v = 60.0 ± 0.1 cm, find max percentage error in f.
  16. Calculate max percentage error in c = Q / (m Δθ) given 2%, 1%, and 3% errors respectively.
  17. Subtract 2.50 g from 10.354 g using significant figure rules.
  18. Find base units of M, L, T if Force=20N, Energy=200J, Velocity=5 m/s are fundamental.
  19. Show that 1 calorie (4.2 J) in a new system is 4.2 α-1 β-2 γ2.
  20. Prove t ∝ √(ρ r3 / σ) for a liquid drop.
  21. Convert 109 light-years into parsecs (1 pc ≈ 3.26 ly).
  22. Convert 100 J into a system with units 20 cm, 250 g, and 30 s.
  23. Find dimensions/SI units of a and b in (P + a/V2)(V - b) = nRT.
  24. Relate critical velocity vc to η, ρ, and r using dimensional analysis.
  25. Mass 2.00 ± 0.01 g, Volume 5.0 ± 0.1 cm3. Find max percentage error in density.
  26. A wire of length L and radius r is stretched by force F. Find dimensions of Young's Modulus Y = (F/A) / (ΔL/L).
  27. If force F, length L, and time T are fundamental, what is the dimensional formula for density?
  28. Calculate the number of significant figures in the following: 0.007, 2.64×1024, 0.2370, 6.320, 6.000.
  29. The equation of a wave is y = A sin(ωt - kx). Find dimensions of ω and k.
  30. Find the percentage error in the area of a circle if the radius is measured with an error of 2%.
