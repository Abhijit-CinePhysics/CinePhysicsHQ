Worksheet 1

Chapter: Units and Measurements

Learning Outcomes

  1. Understand the essential need for measurement in physical sciences and how it establishes quantitative laws.
  2. Distinguish between physical and non-physical quantities, and categorize them into fundamental and derived types.
  3. Comprehend the development of various systems of units and the structural framework of the International System of Units (SI).
  4. Apply standard SI guidelines, proper symbols, and base dimensions to evaluate foundational physical constants and expressions.

Instructions

  1. All questions are compulsory. The worksheet is divided into five sections: Section A, Section B, Section C, Section D, and Section E.
  2. Section A contains 10 Multiple Choice Questions (1 mark each). Choose the single best alternative using the automatically lettered options.
  3. Section B contains 4 Assertion-Reason Questions (1 mark each). Evaluate both statements carefully based on standard physical principles.
  4. Section C contains 5 Very Short Answer Questions (1 mark each). Answer in one or two sentences.
  5. Section D contains 5 Short Answer Questions (3 marks each). Show brief step-by-step reasoning where applicable.
  6. Section E contains 4 Numerical Questions (5 marks each). Show the analytical steps clearly, substitute base data, and report values with correct SI units.

Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

  1. Which of the following processes is fundamentally required to verify a quantitative law or theory in physical sciences?
    1. Qualitative sensory observation
    2. Systematic measurement of relevant physical quantities
    3. Theoretical estimation without experimental trial
    4. Philosophical postulation of space and time
  2. A property or characteristic of an object or phenomenon that can be quantified and measured using a standard instrument is defined as a:
    1. Fundamental unit
    2. Derived unit
    3. Physical quantity
    4. System of units
  3. In the historical FPS system of units, the base units designated for the measurement of length, mass, and time are respectively:
    1. Foot, pound, second
    2. Foot, gram, second
    3. Furlong, pound, second
    4. Foot, quintal, second
    5. Foot, slug, second
  4. Which of the following combinations contains only base or fundamental SI units?
    1. Joule, newton, watt
    2. Kilogram, meter, second, ampere
    3. Meter, kilogram, coulomb, kelvin
    4. Radian, steradian, candela, mole
  5. The SI unit of luminous intensity, which measures the perceived power of light emitted by a source in a particular direction per unit solid angle, is the:
    1. Lux
    2. Lumen
    3. Candela
    4. Watt per steradian
  6. A derived unit is best described as a unit that:
    1. Can be defined independently of all other physical units
    2. Is defined through a specific algebraic combination of base fundamental units
    3. Is used exclusively to measure supplementary geometrical properties
    4. Does not depend on any underlying choice of a system of units
  7. Expressing the derived SI unit of force, the newton, purely in terms of fundamental SI base units yields which of the following combinations?
    1. kg m s-1
    2. kg m2 s-2
    3. kg m s-2
    4. kg m-1 s-2
  8. Which of the following options represents a derived physical quantity whose unit can be simplified to kg m2 s-3 in the SI system?
    1. Work done
    2. Mechanical power
    3. Linear momentum
    4. Kinetic energy
  9. What is the fundamental reason for adopting the International System of Units (SI) globally in scientific and commercial domains?
    1. It is based entirely on the arbitrary dimensions of the human body
    2. It is a coherent and rationalized system with universally reproducible standards
    3. It completely eliminates the need to measure very large or very small magnitudes
    4. It uses non-decimal multipliers making manual calculation simpler
  10. If a new system of units is proposed where the unit of mass is 2 kg, the unit of length is 5 m, and the unit of time is 10 s, what will be the numerical value of a 10 N force in this new system?
    1. 2
    2. 5
    3. 10
    4. 20

Section B: Assertion Reason

Directions: Each of the following questions consists of two statements, one labeled as Assertion (A) and the other labeled as Reason (R). Examine the statements carefully and select the correct option according to the following guidelines:

  1. Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).
  2. Both (A) and (R) are true but (R) is not the correct explanation of (A).
  3. (A) is true but (R) is false.
  4. (A) is false but (R) is true.
  1. Assertion (A): Physics is an exact science that relies completely on the precise measurement of physical quantities. Reason (R): Measurement allows scientists to formulate quantitative laws, test theoretical predictions, and maintain universal consistency.
  2. Assertion (A): The number of fundamental units in any chosen system of units must match the total number of all known physical quantities in nature. Reason (R): Derived units are generated by multiplying or dividing fundamental units without introducing new independent base units.
  3. Assertion (A): The unit of electrical thermodynamic temperature in the SI system is the kelvin, which is categorized as a base unit. Reason (R): Fundamental base units are completely independent of one another and cannot be derived from other base units within the system.
  4. Assertion (A): The expression for velocity gradient can be represented by a base SI unit combination of s-1. Reason (R): Velocity gradient is defined as the change in velocity per unit change in temperature.

Section D: Short Answer

  1. Explain the historical limitations of regional unit systems such as the CGS and MKS systems, and discuss why the global scientific community transitioned to a rationalized, coherent system like the SI system.
  2. The density of an object is measured as 8.4 g cm-3 in a legacy laboratory setup. Convert this value systematically into the standard SI system using structural base unit conversions, and show each intermediate step clearly.
  3. State three essential criteria that a physical standard unit must satisfy to be selected as a fundamental base unit under international measurement guidelines.
  4. A student proposes a mechanical model where the derived unit of energy is defined as the product of mass, a characteristic length, and a frequency factor. Evaluate whether this product yields a valid unit configuration for energy in the SI system.
  5. Show explicitly how the derived unit of electric potential, the volt, can be completely reduced to a specific combination of fundamental SI base units (kilogram, meter, second, and ampere).

Section E: Numericals

  1. The velocity of an acoustic wave traveling through a gas medium depends on its pressure and its mass density. If the pressure of the gas is measured as 1.01 × 105 kg m-1 s-2 and its mass density is 1.29 kg m-3, calculate the numerical value of the ratio of pressure to density. State the final derived unit of this specific ratio in proper SI superscript format.
  2. A experimental variable y is constructed by multiplying a force value by a time value and then dividing the product by a length scale. If a force of 45.0 N is applied over a duration of 2.0 s on an object across a spatial displacement length of 3.0 m, calculate the quantitative value of y. Reduce its unit to its lowest fundamental SI base configuration.
  3. An asset tracking system measures the volumetric flow rate of a liquid through a primary delivery conduit. The measured rate is recorded as 250.0 L min-1. Convert this volumetric flow rate into standard SI base units of m3 s-1, given that 1 L = 10-3 m3. Show the conversion factors explicitly.
  4. The mechanical power P developed by an engine component is given by the relation P = F v, where F is the applied force and v is the instantaneous velocity. If the force acting on the component is 2.50 × 103 kg m s-2 and it moves with an instantaneous velocity of 12.0 m s-1, compute the total power output. Verify that the resulting unit matches the base combinations of the watt (W).
