Worksheet 3

Class 11 Physics Practice Worksheet

Chapter: Units and Measurements Selected Topics: Dimensions, Dimensional Formulae, Dimensional Analysis, and Applications of Dimensional Analysis Difficulty Level: Mixed (CBSE & NCERT aligned)

Learning Outcomes

  1. Determine the dimensional formulae of foundational physical quantities.
  2. Apply the principle of homogeneity of dimensions to validate physical equations.
  3. Utilize dimensional analysis to convert units between systems and derive relationships between physical variables.

Instructions

  1. Answer all questions using proper scientific terminology and formatting.
  2. Show step-by-step substitution of dimensions for numerical and short answer questions.
  3. Verify that the physical equations maintain dimensional consistency.

Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

  1. Which of the following pairs of physical quantities shares identical dimensional formulae?
    1. Work and Torque
    2. Force and Power
    3. Stress and Strain
    4. Impulse and Momentum Gradient
  2. The dimensional formula for the universal gravitational constant G is:
    1. [M-1 L3 T-2]
    2. [M1 L3 T-2]
    3. [M-1 L2 T-2]
    4. [M-2 L3 T-1]
  3. In the relation P = a / x + b t, where P represents pressure, x denotes distance, and t represents time, the dimensions of the constant coefficient b must be:
    1. [M1 L-1 T-3]
    2. [M1 L-2 T-2]
    3. [M1 L1 T-3]
    4. [M0 L2 T-1]
  4. If force F, velocity v, and time t are chosen as the fundamental base quantities instead of mass, length, and time, then the dimensional formula for mass will be expressed as:
    1. [F v-1 t1]
    2. [F v1 t-1]
    3. [F v-1 t-1]
    4. [F v2 t-1]
  5. According to the principle of homogeneity of dimensions, an equation is considered dimensionally correct if:
    1. The dimensions of every term on both sides of the equation are equal.
    2. The total numerical values on both sides of the equation are equal.
    3. The arguments of trigonometric functions contain dimensions of length.
    4. The constant of proportionality is equal to one.
  6. The dimensional analysis method cannot be applied to derive a physical relation if the dependent variable depends on more than:
    1. Three independent physical variables involving mass, length, and time.
    2. Two independent physical variables involving mass and length.
    3. Four independent dimensionless scaling constants.
    4. One single fundamental base dimension.
  7. The dimensional formula for the coefficient of viscosity μ is:
    1. [M1 L-1 T-1]
    2. [M1 L-2 T-1]
    3. [M1 L-1 T-2]
    4. [M1 L1 T-1]
  8. The expression for the velocity of a particle is given by v = v0 e-k t, where t represents time. The dimensional formula for the constant factor k is:
    1. [M0 L0 T-1]
    2. [M0 L0 T1]
    3. [M1 L0 T-1]
    4. [M0 L1 T-1]
  9. Which of the following parameters represents a dimensionless physical quantity that still possesses a specific unit of measurement?
    1. Plane Angle (θ)
    2. Relative Density
    3. Refractive Index
    4. Strain
  10. If kinetic energy E depends on the mass m of an object and its speed v as E = k mx vy, dimensional analysis yields which values for the exponents x and y?
    1. x = 1, y = 2
    2. x = 2, y = 1
    3. x = 1, y = 1
    4. x = 1, y = -2

Section B: Assertion Reason

  1. Assertion: A physical equation that is dimensionally correct may not necessarily be a physically valid or correct equation. Reason: Dimensionless constants present in a physical relation cannot be determined or validated solely by the method of dimensional analysis.
  2. Assertion: The quantity velocity gradient has the same dimensional formula as frequency. Reason: Velocity gradient is defined as the change in velocity per unit displacement.
  3. Assertion: Arguments of logarithmic, exponential, and trigonometric functions used in physical equations must always be dimensionless. Reason: The properties of pure mathematical ratios require that their inputs do not carry fundamental base dimensions.
  4. Assertion: The unit system conversion factor from SI to CGS for work is 107. Reason: The dimensional formula of work contains a length dimension squared, which scales by a factor of 1002 during unit transformation.
  5. Assertion: You can add a velocity vector term to an acceleration vector term if they act along the same line of motion. Reason: Only physical quantities possessing identical dimensions can be added, subtracted, or compared.

Section C: Very Short Answer

  1. Define the term 'Dimensions of a physical quantity' using clear scientific terminology.
  2. State the fundamental limitation of dimensional analysis regarding equations that involve a sum or difference of multiple terms, such as Δx = v0 t + 0.5 a t2.
  3. Write down the dimensional formula for the physical parameter kinetic energy and compare it directly with the dimensional formula for potential energy.
  4. Identify the dimensional formula of the ratio of linear momentum to angular momentum for a rotating particle.
  5. An angle θ is measured as the ratio of an arc length to the radius of a circle. Explain briefly why this parameter is classified as a dimensionless quantity.

Section D: Short Answer

  1. Explain the principle of homogeneity of dimensions. How is this principle utilized to verify whether a newly formulated physical equation is correct or incorrect?
  2. The viscous force F acting on a small spherical ball of radius r falling through a fluid depends on the coefficient of viscosity μ, the radius r, and the terminal velocity v. Use the method of dimensional analysis to establish the functional relationship between these quantities.
  3. Describe three major structural limitations of the applications of dimensional analysis when analyzing complex physical frameworks.
  4. The velocity v of water waves depends on the wavelength λ, the density of water ρ, and the acceleration due to gravity g. Deduce the relation between v, λ, ρ, and g using dimensional analysis rules.
  5. A student writes a formula for the time period of a simple pendulum as T = 2 π √(g / l), where l is the length and g is the acceleration due to gravity. Check the dimensional consistency of this equation and state whether the formula is correct or incorrect.

Section E: Numericals

  1. The value of the acceleration due to gravity is measured as 9.8 m s-2 in the SI system. Convert this numerical value into a new custom unit system where the base unit of length is defined as 1 km and the base unit of time is defined as 1 minute.
  2. In a specific experimental setup, the pressure P of a gas depends on volume V and temperature T according to the van der Waals expression (P + a / V2)(V - b) = R T. Determine the explicit dimensional formulae for the custom constant parameters a and b.
  3. The frequency of oscillation f of a stretched string depends on its hanging length l, the tension force F applied to the string, and its mass per unit length m. Derive the exact proportional formula for frequency utilizing dimensional analysis parameters.
  4. Convert a energy value of 100 Joules (SI unit) into Ergs (CGS unit) by implementing the formal dimensional analysis unit conversion formula n2 = n1 [M1/M2]a [L1/L2]b [T1/T2]c.
  5. The rate of flow of a liquid through a capillary tube, measured as volume per unit time V/t, depends on the pressure gradient across the tube (P/l), the radius of the inner core r, and the fluid coefficient of viscosity μ. Derive the formula for the fluid flow rate using the principles of dimensional analysis.
