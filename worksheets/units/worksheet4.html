Class 11 Physics Numerical Practice Worksheet

Chapter: Units and Measurements Selected Topics: Systems of Units, SI Units, Significant Figures, Errors in Measurement, Uncertainty, and Dimensional Analysis Difficulty Level: Mixed (CBSE & NCERT aligned)

Learning Outcomes

  1. Convert physical quantities across different unit systems using dimensional analysis.
  2. Evaluate and propagate absolute, relative, and percentage errors through complex algebraic calculations.
  3. Apply the rules of significant figures to compute high-precision scientific measurements.
  4. Determine unknown parameters and constants within physical equations using the principle of dimensional homogeneity.

Instructions

  1. Solve all numerical problems showing step-by-step calculations and intermediate formulas.
  2. State your final answer with proper SI units and the correct number of significant figures.
  3. Maintain dimensional consistency across all derived steps.

Section E: Numericals

  1. The length, breadth, and thickness of a rectangular block of wood are measured as 12.05 cm, 8.12 cm, and 3.25 cm respectively. Calculate the total surface area and volume of the block to the correct number of significant figures.
  2. An experimenter measures the period of oscillation of a simple pendulum. In successive measurements, the readings are recorded as 2.63 s, 2.56 s, 2.42 s, 2.71 s, and 2.80 s. Calculate the mean value of the period, the mean absolute error, the relative error, and the percentage error.
  3. The value of universal gravitational constant G in the SI system is 6.67 × 10-11 N m2 kg-2. Use dimensional analysis to convert its value into the CGS system of units in dyne cm2 g-2.
  4. A physical quantity P is given by the relation P = a3 b2 / (c1/2 d). If the percentage errors of measurement in physical quantities a, b, c, and d are 1%, 2%, 3%, and 4% respectively, determine the maximum percentage error possible in the calculated quantity P.
  5. The initial and final temperatures of an oil bath are measured to be θ1 = 24.6 °C ± 0.3 °C and θ2 = 68.9 °C ± 0.4 °C. Calculate the net rise in temperature of the bath along with its proper absolute error boundary.
  6. The resistance R of a wire segment is given by R = V / I, where the potential difference V = 120 ± 6 V and the current strength I = 12 ± 0.4 A. Find the total percentage error in the measurement of resistance R.
  7. The acceleration due to gravity g at a place is calculated using a simple pendulum via the formula g = 4 π2 L / T2. If the measured length L is 90.0 cm ± 0.1 cm and the total time for 100 oscillations is measured as 180.0 s ± 0.6 s with a stopwatch, find the maximum percentage error in the calculation of g.
  8. According to the van der Waals gas equation, (P + a / V2)(V - b) = R T, where P is the pressure, V is the volume, T is the temperature, and R is the universal gas constant. Find the dimensional formulae of the constant parameters a and b.
  9. The value of a surface tension force is 72 dyne cm-1 in the CGS unit system. Convert this mechanical value into its corresponding value in the SI unit system (N m-1) using dimensional conversion methods.
  10. A specialized vehicle covers a displacement Δx = 240.0 m ± 0.6 m during a time interval of 12.0 s ± 0.3 s. Compute its average velocity value and state the absolute uncertainty associated with this derived velocity.
  11. In an experiment to determine the density of a metallic sphere, the mass m is measured as 4.24 g ± 0.02 g and the radius r is measured as 1.325 cm ± 0.005 cm. Calculate the density of the sphere along with its maximum percentage error.
  12. The velocity v of sound waves through a gaseous medium depends on its pressure P and density ρ. Using the method of dimensional analysis, deduce the physical formula for the velocity of sound in terms of pressure and density.
  13. The mass of a piece of solid iron is found to be 32.42 g and its volume is measured as 4.1 cm3. Find the density of the iron piece up to the mathematically correct number of significant figures.
  14. A force F is applied over a square plate of side length L. If the percentage error in the measurement of force F is 4% and the percentage error in measuring the side length L is 2%, calculate the maximum percentage error in the estimation of the pressure exerted on the plate.
  15. The kinetic energy E of a rotating flywheel depends on its moment of inertia I and its angular velocity ω. Assuming the relation is of exponential power form, use dimensional analysis to derive the formula for kinetic energy E.
  16. A student adds three different lengths recorded during an optical bench experiment: 2.345 m, 0.12 m, and 5.6 m. State the final total length of the experimental layout up to the correct number of significant figures.
  17. The electrical capacity C of a capacitor is defined by C = Q / V, where Q is charge and V is potential difference. Given that the dimensional formula for charge Q is [A T] and potential difference V is [M L2 T-3 A-1], find the dimensional formula of capacity C.
  18. A professional stopwatch used in a track event has a least count of 0.02 s. If the time for 50 laps is recorded as 200.00 s, determine the absolute uncertainty, the relative uncertainty, and the final percentage error in this time measurement.
  19. Find the value of a 100 Watt power source in a new system of units where the base units of mass, length, and time are chosen to be 100 g, 20 cm, and 30 s respectively.
  20. The heat generated in a current-carrying wire is given by H = I2 R t. If the percentage errors in measuring the current I, electrical resistance R, and time t are 2%, 1%, and 1.5% respectively, calculate the maximum percentage error in the evaluation of total heat generated.
  21. The velocity v of a particle at time t is given by the non-linear expression v = a t + b / (t + c). Determine the precise dimensional formulae for the physical constants a, b, and c.
  22. The radius of a solid copper cylinder is measured as r = 2.02 ± 0.01 cm and its length is measured as h = 10.14 ± 0.04 cm. Calculate the total volume of the cylinder and the absolute error in the calculated volume value.
  23. An unknown force function is given by F = A sin(B t) + C cos(D x), where t represents time and x represents displacement. Deduce the dimensional formulae of the physical ratios A/B and C/D.
  24. A cube has a measured side length of 4.123 m. Compute the total surface area of all six faces and the volume contained by the cube, rounding off your calculations to the correct number of significant figures.
  25. The mass of an astronaut is 75.0 kg. If a relativistic mass correction is applied where mass varies with velocity, check if the formula m = m0 / √(1 - v2) is dimensionally correct, where v is velocity and m0 is rest mass. Adjust it dimensionally if it is found to be incorrect.
  26. A precise capillary tube experiment shows that the height h of a liquid column depends on the surface tension S, the radius of the tube r, the density of the liquid ρ, and the acceleration due to gravity g. Deduce the proportional formula for h using dimensional analysis.
  27. Calculate the absolute value of the expression 4.32 × 103 m minus 2.4 × 102 m, and state the final displacement value with the correct number of significant figures.
  28. The magnetic force acting on a moving charge is given by F = q v B, where q is charge with dimensions [A T], v is velocity with dimensions [L T-1], and F is force with dimensions [M L T-2]. Determine the correct dimensional formula for the magnetic field strength B.
  29. A physical quantity Y is defined by the function Y = log(c x2 / t), where x is displacement and t is time. Determine the dimensional formula of the constant parameter c required to make the argument of the logarithm function valid.
  30. The terminal velocity v of a spherical ball dropping through a viscous fluid column depends on the radius of the ball r, the acceleration due to gravity g, and the coefficient of viscosity μ of the fluid. Derive the relationship between v, r, g, and μ using the method of dimensional analysis.
